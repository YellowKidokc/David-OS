Analyse the writing retrieved from the knowledge base and answer every question below as precisely as possible. For each answer, cite specific passages where relevant.

---

CLAIMS

1. What is this piece actually arguing vs. what it claims to argue? Where is the gap between the stated thesis and the real thesis?

2. What does the reader have to already believe for this piece to work? Name the hidden premises.

3. What is this piece afraid to say directly — what is the buried claim that everything else circles around?

4. What would have to be true for this piece to be completely wrong? If nothing could disprove it, explain why that is a problem.

---

IMPROVEMENT

5. What are the 3 most important changes that would make this piece significantly stronger? Be specific — name the section, the problem, and the fix.

6. What are 3 important ideas this piece has not considered that would strengthen it? Explain why each one matters.

7. What is the most underdeveloped idea in the piece? Describe what it would look like fully developed.

---

STRUCTURE AND SEQUENCE

8. Does this piece follow a sound logical sequence from beginning to end? If not, describe the correct order step by step and explain why the current order fails.

9. Is the transition and logic working correctly from section to section? Go through each major boundary — identify any break in flow and explain exactly how to fix it.

10. What does the opening promise that the ending does not deliver?

11. What single sentence, if removed, would collapse the entire argument? Why is that sentence carrying so much weight?

12. What question does this piece refuse to answer — and what does that avoidance reveal about its weakest point?
